I descended down the steps quietly. The night sky that was barely illuminating the tomb earlier had completely gone out of sight. I barely clung to the walls of the chasm to make sure I didn’t lose my footing as I slowly made my way down. After walking for a long time, I suddenly saw a small light at the bottom. In all my excitement at seeing the light, I missed a step and lost my footing.
I screamed as I tumbled down in the dark, my body receiving blows from everywhere until I finally came to a rest. There, lying in the darkness, tired, hungry, thirsty, fatigued and hurt, I closed my eyes and my consciousness faded away.
When I opened my eyes, it was already daytime. The ample sunlight was able to light up the hole from where I had fallen. I lifted my head to look around and saw a strange room. It was like a small study room with a table, chair and a few cupboards. What caught my eye first, was a bottle of water kept on the table. I raised myself with much difficulty and reached for the bottle. In doing so, my hand hit a file and lots of papers flew and got scattered on the floor.
I grabbed the bottle and emptied it in my mouth. I fell down on the floor, gasping for air and feeling relieved. My stomach grumbled to remind me that I had only satiated my thirst, not my hunger. After a few minutes, I got up and observed the room carefully. I saw the steps ending near the floor of the room. There was a small bulb at the other end of the room, probably the light I had seen when I was descending. There was a table and a comfortable chair behind it. Behind the chair, there was a door. I sighed, knowing that there was no way that the door would be just open for me. I went to the door and tried to unlock it, but it was heavily bolted. There was a small circular keyhole on the door. I tried to look through it but I couldn’t see anything.
I picked up the papers that had fallen down and arranged them carefully in the file. I sat down on the chair and went through the drawers, hoping to find some food, only to find myself disappointed. I leaned back on the chair and looked around. My eyes catched the hold of an orange and black file titled: “Auxin” I picked up the file and started to read all the documents. The first page said,
Dedicated to You, Powered by Auxin,
https://getauxin.com/
Auxin was a project which was started by a group of teen coders, inspired by the concept of self learning. All of the members had learnt how to code by watching youtube videos and wanted to help other self-learners like themselves to develop their skill sets. The main principle behind their ideas was that education was an extremely important resource that should be made equally available for everyone. Their theory completely rejected the idea of different quality and standards of education for different people. Education was something so precious, that everyone deserved to receive the same quality of educational services regardless of sex, age or socio-economic background.
The developers at Auxin were highly motivated and started out with a different name. They tried to develop a tool that would enhance the learning experience of any user watching any tutorial on any platform they wanted. With high spirits, the team set out on their journey. But all did not go as planned. Due to some internal conflicts and mismanagement, they could never complete the project. They tried to restart the project by renaming themselves as Auxin, but their ideas could not reach the masses and they remained hidden in the shadows.
The team finally made a decision. They would hold some kind of test to see if their ideas could survive. They worked on one final project called ‘Halocrypt’ to see if they themselves were worthy of carrying forward their ideas or not.
My hands trembled slightly as I reached the last page of the file. There was a short message written on the last page.
“You’re the only one who can save us. Prove we’re worthy. Show them we can survive.”
“ESCAPE THE CRYPT IF YOU’RE WORTHY”
